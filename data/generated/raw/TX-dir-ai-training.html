What is a Certified Artificial Intelligence (AI) Awareness Training Program? 


Texas Government Code Section 2054.5193 requires the Department of Information Resources (DIR), in consultation with the Public Sector AI Systems Advisory Board, to certify at least five AI training programs for state and local government employees.  Furthermore, Government Code Section 2054.5191 mandates that state and local government employees and officials complete a certified AI awareness training program.

Annual Training Requirements  


State and local government entities are required to train their employees annually on a certified training program. 


Employees required to complete the training are outlined in the table below. 






Entity Type 
Training Required For 
Training Due Date 




State agencies* 


    

  • Employees who use a computer to complete at least 25 percent of the employee's required duties
    • 

  • Elected or appointed officers of the agency  
    • 



Annually 




Local government entities 


    

  • Employees who have access to a local government computer system or database and use a computer to perform at least 25 percent of the employee's required duties
    • 

  • Elected or appointed officials who have access to a local government computer system or database and use a computer to perform at least 25 percent of the official's required duties
    • 

  • For school districts, only the district's cybersecurity coordinator is required to complete annual AI training.  Any other school district employee required to complete the AI training shall complete the training as determined by the district, with the district's cybersecurity coordinator. Elected officials are subject to local government training requirements.  
    • 



Annually 






*The term state agency, as defined in Chapter 2054 of Government Code, includes a department, commission, board, office, council, authority, or other entity within the executive or judicial branch of state government that is created by the constitution or by statute. This definition also includes university systems and institutions of higher education, as defined by Education Code Section 61.003.


Exceptions to Training Requirements 


The training requirements do not apply to employees and officials who have been: 


    

  • Granted military leave;
    • 

  • Granted leave under the federal Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (29 U.S.C Section 2601 et seq.);
    • 

  • Granted leave related to a sickness or disability covered by workers' compensation benefits, if that employee no longer has access to the state agency's or local government entity's database and systems;
    • 

  • Granted any other type of extended leave or authorization to work from an alternative work site if that employee no longer has access to the state agency's or local government entity's database and systems; or
    • 

  • Denied access to a local government entity's computer system or database by the governing body of the local government entity or the governing body's designee for noncompliance with the training requirements.  
    • 



Reporting Training 


Government entities must annually certify their compliance with the training requirements by August 31, using the Cybersecurity and Artificial Intelligence Training Certification for State and Local Governments. 


Government entities can track their compliance in any method they choose, and do not submit training records or employee certificates of completion to DIR.  


Training Programs

The list of certified training programs for FY 25-26 is below and valid until August 31, 2026. Please note that these programs are certified for content, not other regulatory or statutory obligations.


Download the Certified Training Programs


The list of certified training programs for FY 25-26 will be updated as additional training programs are certified.


DIR Training Programs 


DIR has developed a certified AI awareness training program, which is available at no cost. This training is intended for individuals who are required to comply with the provisions of Government Code Sections 2054.5191 and based on the requirements of their respective organization. Please note that this training does not include tracking capabilities or provide certificates of completion. Accordingly, employers are responsible for documenting their employees’ completion of the training using a method of their choosing.


FY 25-26 Texas AI Awareness Training


Training Program Certification 


Government Code Section 2054.5193(b) states that an artificial intelligence training program must: 


    

  1. focus on forming an understanding of how artificial intelligence technology may be used in relation to a state employee's responsibilities and duties; and
    2. 

  2. teach best practices on literacy in deploying and operating the artificial intelligence technologies. 
    3. 



Section 2054.5193(a)(3) requires DIR to ensure that the artificial intelligence training programs are equal in length to the cybersecurity training programs it certifies. DIR certifies cybersecurity training programs of varying lengths and will do the same for artificial intelligence training programs. Government entities are responsible for ensuring compliance with this provision.  


DIR, in consultation with the Public Sector AI Systems Advisory Board, publishes criteria that training programs must meet to be certified. 


FY 25-26 Training Program Certification Standards Final.pdf 


There is no cost to have a training program reviewed for certification.  Certifications are valid until August 31 and need to be renewed annually. 


Training Program Certification Request 


Applications for FY 25-26 training program certifications are now available and will be accepted on an ongoing basis.  There are 2 types of applications.  Review the details below and submit the appropriate application.  A disposition notification will be provided within 30 days of application submission.


    

  • Submit a Training Program Certification for Government Programs (This form is for public sector entities seeking certification for a program)
    • 

  • Submit a Training Program Certification Application for Vendor Programs (This form is for vendors seeking certification for a program.)
